Project Proposal: Skincare Routine

Problem/Data Domain

The definition of skincare and a skin care routine will vary between people as it reflects their individual skin goals. For example, a skincare regime can range from a 2 step-process of face wash and moisturizer to an extensive 8-step process that rotates day and night. Skincare products serve various purposes, from hydrating the skin to addressing long-term skin-damage. A standard skin care routine typically includes products such as cleansers, moisturizers, toners, sunscreens and exfoliants. More specialized products are also available to further meet an individual's specific skin goals and concerns.

The practice of skin care has a history spanning thousands of years, marked by numerous industry transformations that have brought new and traditional knowledge. However, this abundance of information and exponential increase in available skincare products, has left consumers feeling overwhelmed by choices. Many companies profit on this confusion by marketing their products as universally effective, but this one-size-fits-all approach often falls short. An effective skin care routines must be personalized to align with an individual's preferences, lifestyle, and skin needs.

An existing attempt to solve this problem has been done by e.l.f. Cosmetics, a cosmetics brand based in Oakland California. This company has created a short quiz that asks the user questions about their skin type and daily skincare routine needs in order to provide users with recommendations for different products they can use to meet their skin care needs. A limitation of this approach is that it focuses on advertising e.l.f. products and thus may be biased in terms of promoting its own products and making a profit.

The users will begin by taking a quiz (small feature) that will ask them questions about their skin type. The users will then be shown a page that will contain information about different products that are recommended to them based on their answers. The user will also be able to filter out specific product suggestions based on brand, price, and ratings (significant feature). From there, they will be able to organize and group products together into a routine based on their skincare needs (significant feature). The user will be prevented from grouping products together that should not be used at the same time.
